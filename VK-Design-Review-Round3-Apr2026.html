Co-op Tiles — itile System
Open
Design Rationale
The mega menu has three co-op zones: Zone 1 is the right-panel Tier 1 spotlight (brand-funded, single product, single message, rotates per category). Zone 2 is the bottom trust strip. Zone 3 is the itile row — a flexible tile format with photo left, editorial copy right, hover zoom, and arrow indicator.

The itile system supports 3–4 rotating format options per category: photo-led (full bleed image, minimal text), text-led (dark background, copy-forward, no photo dependency), split editorial (image + headline + short descriptor), and spotlight (single product with pricing callout). This gives VK the flexibility to match the tile format to the brand and moment without rebuilding the zone. Open question for discussion: which format mix is right per category, and how often do tiles rotate.

Migration SEO Risk + Integrations Subdomain
Decided
Design Rationale
Migration SEO risk acknowledged and factored into agency selection and launch timing. On the Integrations subdomain: integration.vintageking.com currently operates as a separate website from vintageking.com. Search engines treat them as two distinct properties — so backlinks, authority signals, and trust built up on one do not automatically benefit the other. This splits what should be a single, authoritative domain into two weaker ones. Consolidating VK Integrations into vintageking.com as a Studio Integrations hub page resolves this: all domain authority flows to one property, the customer journey stays unbroken, and the SEO signal for both the main site and the Integrations content strengthens. This is a meaningful improvement that costs nothing extra to execute during the migration. Plan: bring back to main site at or fast-follow after migration, rework and deduplicate content, host client stories on the blog with unique categories and tagging.

The Design Philosophy
The VK Customer Lifecycle — Every Entry Point Has a Path Forward

VK's design is built to invite every serious buyer in and grow them over time. The aspiring buyer who is getting serious today becomes the engineer who calls VK for a console in three years. Every decision in this redesign — the category header split, the buyer guide card, the Ask button, the Rare Gear section, the Studio Installations hub — maps to a specific moment in this journey. The goal is that no buyer at any stage feels intimidated or out of place. They feel invited, guided, and progressively pulled toward the next level.

Entry
Just Getting Serious
AOV ~$500–$1K · Aspiring buyer
Technically sophisticated but still building their setup. Researches before buying. Knows what they want to achieve, not always which gear gets them there. Needs to feel invited, not filtered out.
VK touch: Warm entry points at top of category pages — "Building your first home studio." "First time buying a mic." PLAYBACK Magazine. Buyer guides. Ask button as first rep conversation.
Core
The Working Professional
AOV $1K–$3K · Primary audience
Recording engineer or serious hobbyist. Knows exactly what they want, shops on spec. Time is valuable. Needs fast paths, expert validation, and authority signals at the moment of decision.
VK touch: Mega menu straight to category, filter system, before/during/after stats section, sticky Add to Cart, instant Live Chat. Every second of friction costs a sale.
Growth
The Committed Engineer
AOV $4K–$15K · Recurring buyer
Buying consoles, outboard racks, monitoring systems. Has a rep relationship. Expects white-glove service, financing options, trade-in value, and fast access to rare and vintage gear.
VK touch: Rare Gear editorial, Vintage and Used dropdown (By Era), Sell or Trade, financing/Section 179, extended warranty, pre-order with deposit.
Partner
The Studio Builder
AOV $15K–$100K+ · Full installation
Building or renovating a professional studio. VK is the trusted partner for the full scope — design, procurement, installation, treatment. A single project can span months and define the relationship for years.
VK touch: Studio Installations hub, lightbox intake form, Studio Design dropdown, VK Integrations consolidated on-site. The destination — not the entry — of the VK relationship.
